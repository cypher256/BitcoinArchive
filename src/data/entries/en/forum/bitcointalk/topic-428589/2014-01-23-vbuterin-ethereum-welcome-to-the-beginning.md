On January 23, 2014, [Vitalik Buterin](/BitcoinArchive/participants/vitalik-buterin/) (BitcoinTalk handle `vbuterin`) opened a thread on the BitcoinTalk **Alternative cryptocurrencies / Announcements (Altcoins)** board with the title `Ethereum: Welcome to the Beginning`. The thread is the principal Bitcoin-community-facing record of the Ethereum whitepaper publication, posted two days before Buterin's public announcement at the North American Bitcoin Conference in Miami (January 25, 2014).

The original post linked to the Ethereum whitepaper hosted on `ethereum.org` and described the project as a Turing-complete scripting platform that built on the consensus-and-incentive design of Bitcoin while removing what the post characterized as Bitcoin Script's restrictions on general-purpose computation. The thread accumulated discussion and replies through 2014–2015 from Bitcoin community members, including substantial early scrutiny and skepticism.

The Bitcoin-side significance recorded in this archive: Buterin had been an active Bitcoin community member since 2011 (Bitcoin Magazine co-founder, `pybitcointools` author, BitcoinTalk member from October 2011), and this thread is the moment at which his post-Bitcoin-Magazine work was formally introduced to the same community he had been writing for. The [fork-and-altcoin genealogy analysis](/BitcoinArchive/entries/analysis/2008-10-31-bitcoin-fork-and-altcoin-genealogy/) treats Ethereum as the most-cited "next-generation" chain whose origin runs *through* Bitcoin; this BitcoinTalk thread is the immediate-record document of that origin running through Bitcoin's principal forum.
