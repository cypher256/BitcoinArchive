Peter Todd (born 1985, Canada) is a cryptographer, applied cryptography consultant, and Bitcoin Core developer. He is known for his focus on Bitcoin protocol security, scalability, and his outspoken views on technical trade-offs.

**Bitcoin Core Contributions:**
Todd joined the Bitcoin community in early 2011 via BitcoinTalk and became an active Bitcoin Core contributor. His work focused on protocol-level security, transaction policy, and network resilience. He contributed to various aspects of the codebase and was a vocal participant in technical debates about Bitcoin's direction.

**Replace-by-Fee (RBF):**
Todd is best known for championing Replace-by-Fee (RBF), a transaction replacement policy that allows unconfirmed transactions to be replaced by new versions with higher fees. The concept was formalized in BIP 125 (Opt-in Full Replace-by-Fee Signaling), co-authored by David A. Harding and Peter Todd. RBF was controversial because it made it easier to reverse unconfirmed transactions, but proponents argued it improved fee market efficiency and was a more honest representation of the security model for unconfirmed transactions.

**OpenTimestamps:**
Todd created OpenTimestamps, an open-source project that uses the Bitcoin blockchain to create tamper-proof timestamps. The system allows anyone to prove that a document existed at a particular point in time by anchoring cryptographic commitments into Bitcoin transactions.

**HBO Documentary (October 2024):**
In October 2024, the HBO documentary "Money Electric: The Bitcoin Mystery" named Todd as a candidate for Satoshi Nakamoto's true identity. The documentary pointed to a 2010 BitcoinTalk post where a user appeared to continue a thought from one of Satoshi's messages about transaction replacement. Todd denied the claim, calling it irresponsible and dangerous.

**No Direct Communication with Satoshi:**
Todd registered on BitcoinTalk after Satoshi Nakamoto's final post on December 12, 2010. There is no known direct communication between Todd and Satoshi.
